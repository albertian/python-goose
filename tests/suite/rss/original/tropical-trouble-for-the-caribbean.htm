Tropical trouble for the Caribbean

Posted: Updated:

BY JIM REIF

Tropical Storm Sandy will not be coming to SW Florida. But her planned itinerary through the Caribbean and the Bahamas means big trouble for a number of island nations to our South.

Hurricane warnings are posted for Jamaica and Eastern Cuba as this strengthening storm starts to accelerate toward the N and NE.

Haiti will be on the rainy side of this storm's track and the thought of 5-10 inches of rain on a poor nation prone to life-threatening mud slides is quite scary.

The Bahamas may escape hurricane force winds, but flooding rains and high tides coupled with 60-70 mph winds may ruin much more than just vacation plans.

On this seventh anniversary week of Hurricane Wilma's arrival, Florida will get off relatively easy this time around.

Sandy will make its closest approach on Friday.

Florida's West Coast will be brushed with 20-30 mph winds with a few higher gusts, while the East Coast gets some rain squalls and some nastier gusts between 40-50 mph.
